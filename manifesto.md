## üîπSissel Tolaas-NASALO
Sissel Tolaas is a Norwegian artist, chemist, and olfactory researcher. NASALO is a "smell alphabet" created by Tolaas after 20 years of experiments, with labels based on objective data like molecular structure and volatility, avoiding "good/bad" dichotomies. Each word is an original fantasy combination (e.g., consonant clusters), and experiments show labels directly influence perception (e.g., a "cheese" label makes body odor more pleasant).

## üîπChinese
Chinese language has moderate to low capabilities in describing smells, primarily relying on concrete metaphors and binary oppositions, lacking an abstract dedicated vocabulary system.
¬∑Vocabulary Characteristics
Chinese smell words are mainly source-oriented, categorized into two major dimensions:
Food-related: Fruit scents (banana, watermelon), food and drinks (cake, milk), meal seasonings (fishy, stinky tofu, soy sauce, vinegar).
Non-food-related: Plant and flower scents (tea, mint, jasmine), man-made objects (gasoline, leather, cigarette, disinfectant), natural objects (earth).
Core specific words are limited: fragrant/foul (binary), fishy (fish/shrimp), gamey (mutton), urinous (urine/fox), musty (damp rot), burnt (scorching).
¬∑Semantic and Perceptual Organization
Semantic space: Organized by "food-related/non-food-related" and "man-made/natural" axes, prototype-driven (e.g., fruit scents cluster as sweet fragrance).
Perceptual dimensions: Pleasantness + intensity, divided into sweet-fragrant, disliked, and repulsive smells, but classification consistency is less precise than color terms.
Metaphorical extension: "Flavor" (gunpowder flavor = tension) borrows smell to metaphorize abstract traits.

Compared to Jahai/Maniq's 12+ abstract smell words or NASALO's objective labels, Chinese is more poetic but less precise, suited for literary layering (e.g., "fermented tofu broth's subtle mustiness"), reflecting agricultural culture's prioritization of vision/taste.

## üîπperfumery, coffee, and wine industries
use specialized Flavor/Perfumery Wheels and layered fragrance systems to transform complex olfactory experiences into standardized, multidimensional language. The Flavor/Perfumery Wheel serves as an industry-standard circular classification tool for scents, emphasizing continuous transitions and family relationships between fragrance families. In contrast, the Fragrance Pyramid (layered fragrance system) is a pyramid structure built on temporal volatility, describing the evolution of a single perfume's scent over time.

Professional scent wheels and fragrance pyramid systems have limitations, mainly in positive bias (favoring pleasant smells), cultural narrowness, and lack of abstraction, failing to cover the full spectrum of everyday smells.
